Training Principle: Overload

You MUST challenge yourself every week to build mass. Focus on one of these:

  • **Increase Weight:** Use slightly heavier weight.
  • **Increase Reps:** Do more reps within the 8-12 range.
  • **Time Under Tension:** Slow down the lowering (negative) phase to 3-4 seconds.

Nutrition & Supplementation

Strategy: Calorie Deficit for Toning

To achieve fat loss and "toning," maintain a **Slight Calorie Deficit** (300-500 kcal deficit). Crucially, keep protein intake high to spare muscle.

  • **Calorie Focus:** Reduce simple carbs (sugary drinks, white bread, processed snacks) and processed fats.
  • **Carb Timing:** Consume complex carbs (rice, oats, sweet potatoes) mainly around your workouts for energy.

Protein Sources (No Pork or Beef)

Target: **150-177 grams** daily. This is achievable by prioritizing these sources:

  • 1. Protein Powder (Whey/Casein)

    Delivers 20-30g protein for 120-150 calories. Use post-workout (Whey) and potentially before bed (Casein).

  • 2. Poultry & Fish

    Chicken breast, turkey, tuna, salmon (excellent for healthy fats), and cod. Aim for 4-6 oz per main meal.

  • 3. Dairy

    Greek yogurt (0% fat) and cottage cheese. A cup of cottage cheese provides 25-30g protein—perfect snack!

  • 4. Eggs

    Prioritize egg whites (pure protein), mixed with a whole egg for nutrients. A quick, easy protein hit.

  • 5. Plant-Based

    Tofu, edamame, beans, and lentils. Incorporate these into vegetarian meals.

Recommended Supplements

Your ideal stack: **Protein Powder + Creatine Monohydrate**.

  • Creatine (Highly Recommended)

    Increases muscle strength and power during lifts. **Dosage: 5 grams per day, consistently (timing doesn't matter).**

  • Mass Gainers (AVOID)

    These are too high in calories and will work against your goal of reducing body fat to ~13%. Stick to normal protein powder.
